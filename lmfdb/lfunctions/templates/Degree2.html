{{ KNOWL('lfunction', title='L-functions') }} can be organized by {{ KNOWL('lfunction.degree', title='degree') }}. All known {{ KNOWL('lfunction.known_degree2', title='degree 2') }} L-functions have a functional equation of one of the two forms \[ \Lambda(s) := N^{s/2} \Gamma_{\mathbb C}(s+\nu) \cdot L(s) = \varepsilon \overline{\Lambda}(1-s) \] or \[ \Lambda(s) := N^{s/2} \Gamma_{\mathbb R}(s+\delta_1+ i \mu) \Gamma_{\mathbb R}(s+\delta_2- i \mu) \cdot L(s) = \varepsilon \overline{\Lambda}(1-s), \] and an Euler product of the form \[ L(s)= \prod_{p|N} \left(1-a_p p^{-s}\right)^{-1}. \prod_{p\nmid N} \left(1-a_p p^{-s}+\chi(p) p^{-2s}\right)^{-1}. \] Here $N$ is the {{ KNOWL('lfunction.conductor', title='conductor') }} of the L-function, $\varepsilon$ is the {{ KNOWL('lfunction.sign', title='sign')}}, and $\chi$, known as the central character, is a primitive {{ KNOWL('character.dirichlet', title='Dirichlet character') }} of conductor dividing $N$. The functions {{ KNOWL('specialfunction.gammarandc', title='$\Gamma_\R$ and $\Gamma_\C$') }} are the gamma factors. The parameter $\nu$ is a positive integer or half-integer, the parameter $\mu$ is real, and $\delta_j$ is 0 or 1. If the central character is even (or odd) then either $2\nu+1$ or $\delta_1+\delta_2$ is even (or odd).

The above table is intended to illustrate the variety of properties which degree 2 L-functions can have. Several constraints are illustrated, such as:
  • The $\Gamma$-factors determine the parity of the central character.
  • A self-dual degree 2 L-function with nontrivial character must be CM, in particular at least half its prime coefficients $a_p$ must vanish.
  • L-functions of Maass forms are (conjecturally) non-arithmetic unless the Maass form has eigenvalue $\frac14$.
