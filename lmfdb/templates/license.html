Frequently asked questions

Is mathematical data really copyrightable?

Indeed, the individual elements of a dataset of mathematical objects are not copyrightable. However, most jurisdictions provide for copyright on a the collection as a whole. For example,

  • In the European Union, "a database will be protected by copyright if the selection or arrangement of its contents constitute the creator's own intellectual creation."
  • In the United States, databases are protected when "formed by the collection and assembling of preexisting materials or of data that are selected, coordinated, or arranged in such a way that the resulting work as a whole constitutes an original work of authorship."

For example, we believe that completeness claims are protected under these clauses, as are search results for queries. Individual facts about L-functions, modular forms, curves or fields are not. Of course, we encourage you to cite us whenever you use the LMFDB!
